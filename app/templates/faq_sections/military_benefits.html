Military Benefit Questions

What military benefits help pay for college?
The main programs are the Post-9/11 GI Bill, Montgomery GI Bill, and Tuition Assistance (TA). These can help cover tuition, housing, and books depending on your eligibility.
Does the GI Bill cover full tuition?
For public in-state schools, the Post-9/11 GI Bill usually covers full tuition. Private or out-of-state schools may have a yearly cap, which means you may need additional funding.
Do I get paid while going to school?
Yes. The Post-9/11 GI Bill provides a monthly housing allowance (MHA) based on your school location and enrollment status.
Do military benefits pay for books?
Yes. The Post-9/11 GI Bill provides a book stipend of up to $1,000 per year, paid directly to you.
What is Tuition Assistance (TA)?
Tuition Assistance is a program for active-duty service members that helps pay for college courses while serving, usually up to $250 per credit hour.
Can I use GI Bill and Tuition Assistance together?
In some cases, yes. This is called “Top-Up,” but it may reduce the total amount of GI Bill benefits you have available later.
How long do GI Bill benefits last?
Most GI Bill benefits provide up to 36 months of education coverage, which is typically enough for a four-year degree.
Can I transfer my benefits to family members?
Yes, in some cases. You may be able to transfer your GI Bill benefits to a spouse or child if you meet service requirements and complete the transfer while still in the military.
What happens if I drop below full-time?
Your housing allowance may be reduced or stopped if you drop below full-time enrollment. Always check before changing your schedule.
